Determinant

Introduction

The determinant is an index of a squared matrix obtained by the determinant function \( \det: \mathbb{R}^{m\times m} \rightarrow \mathbb{R} \) that provides information about the manner of its transformation, the solutions of the encoded simultaneous and is necessary to find the eigenvalues of the matrix. The calculation of the determinant needs a huge amount of steps increasing with the dimension of the matrix.

Algorithm

The mathematical definition of the determinant function states for a matrix \( A \in \mathbb{R}^{1\times 1} \) the determinant is calculated using \begin{align} \det \left( \left[ \begin{matrix} a_{11} \end{matrix} \right] \right) = a_{11} \end{align} Matrices of dimensions \( A \in \mathbb{R}^{n\times n} \) do not have an explicite formula, but are defined relatively using \begin{align} \det(A) = \sum_{i=1}^n (-1)^{i+j} \cdot a_{ij} \cdot \det(A_{ij}) \end{align} where \(j\) is the index of the column from which the determinant is «developed» and \( A_{ij}\) means the matrix without the \(i\)-th row and the \(j\)-th column. This formula has to be repeated untill the matrices \( A_{ij}\) are reduced to the form \(A_{ij}\in\mathbb{R}^{1\times 1} \).
